📋 OVERVIEW — April 20, 2026

Spring is firing on the Marco–Naples flats this week: snook are staging on outside points, redfish are cruising the shallow flats, and the first waves of tarpon are rolling the beaches ahead of the full-moon swing. Offshore bite stays strong with permit, amberjack, and barracuda holding on the early-season wrecks inside 5 miles.

🎣 Inshore

▲
Snook🔥 HOT
Staging on outside points and passes ahead of the spawn. Live pilchards and paddle tails on a moving tide.
Redfish✅ GOOD
Cruising shallow flats and tight to mangrove roots. Cut bait on the bottom or weedless soft plastics.
Sea Trout✅ GOOD
Reliable on grass flats in 3–6 ft. Popping corks with shrimp producing steady slot-class fish.

🌊 Nearshore (≤5mi)

▲
Permit🔥 HOT
Hundreds stacked on inside wrecks this early season. Live crabs on light spinning tackle are the call.
Amberjack✅ GOOD
Holding over the same structure as permit. Heavy jigs and live baits — expect a fight.
Barracuda✅ GOOD
Aggressive on tube lures burned fast over wrecks and reefs inside 5 miles.

🌿 Backwater

▲
Juvenile Tarpon🔥 HOT
Rolling in the Ten Thousand Islands creeks at first light. Small flies and soft plastics on 30 lb leader.
Mangrove Snapper✅ GOOD
Reliable on shrimp tight to structure in the deeper backcountry holes.
Jack Crevalle✅ GOOD
Blowing up bait schools on calm mornings — topwater plugs producing crushing strikes.
