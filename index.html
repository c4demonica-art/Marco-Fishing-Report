📋 OVERVIEW — APRIL 18, 2026

Mid-April and Marco Island is firing on all cylinders. The tarpon migration is building daily with bigger concentrations arriving each week heading toward the May peak. Snook are active everywhere — backcountry, passes, and beach — with clear water opening up sight fishing. Redfish are peak month right now, April and May being the best of the year. A grand slam is absolutely achievable this week.

🌤️ Water 78–80°F · Gulf blue-green, excellent clarity · Baitfish heavy along beach zone · Tarpon migration building to peak · April = peak redfish month · Sunrise 7:05am / Sunset 7:52pm
🌿

Inshore

▲
Water: 78–80°FGrand slam possibleClear water sight fishing

Full spring bite locked in. Snook, redfish and tarpon all active simultaneously. April is the peak month for redfish. Sight fishing excellent in clear conditions.

Snook🔥 HOT
Outside points, oyster bars, mangrove shorelines, passes, and beach — snook are everywhere. Incredible bite wherever you target them. Beach sight fishing in clear water — approach on trolling motor starting 100+ feet away. Topwater at first light, live pilchards or paddle tails as sun rises. Caxambas Pass on outgoing tide a prime spot.
Redfish🔥 HOT
Peak month — April and May are the best redfish months of the year. Pushing to open flats, points, and island edges throughout Ten Thousand Islands. Tailing fish visible on sun-warmed flats. Gold spoons, live shrimp, paddle tails. Work one shoreline and find multiple species. Don't miss this window.
Spotted Seatrout✅ GOOD
Live shrimp under popping corks over deeper grass patches in Rookery Bay. Soft plastics along current seams also productive. Add one to your slam — grand slam status requires a seatrout.
Pompano✅ GOOD
Sandy flats, channel edges, and beach zone. Small jig tipped with shrimp bounced slowly along the bottom. Tigertail Beach and outer bars producing.
Jack Crevalle🔥 HOT
Flooding the passes and beach zone chasing bait schools. Fast action on jigs and spoons. Find the jacks, find the snook — they travel together.
💡 PRO TIP

Start on backcountry flats at first light for redfish and snook, hit Caxambas Pass on the outgoing tide mid-morning, then beach sight fish for snook when sun gets high. Grand slam before noon is realistic this week.

⚡

Nearshore (within 5 mi)

▲
Range: 1–5 milesBaitfish heavyGoliath ramping up

Baitfish stacked along the beach zone. Both artificial reefs producing fast snapper action. Goliath grouper getting aggressive on nearshore structure as April water temps peak.

Spanish Mackerel🔥 HOT
Thick along beach zone with baitfish from Marco to Naples. Fast-retrieve spoons or flashy jigs along the surface. Early morning best before afternoon wind builds. Often mixed with kingfish in the same zone.
Mangrove Snapper🔥 HOT
Both artificial reefs (1mi and 5mi from Caxambas Pass in 20–30ft) loaded. Anchor up, chum with glass minnows, drop live shrimp on light fluorocarbon. Quick limit — great table fare. Fish the first hour of incoming tide when current isn't ripping.
Goliath Grouper🔥 HOT
Ramping up April into early summer on nearshore wrecks inside 5 miles. Warmer water triggering aggressive behavior. Large cut bait or live pinfish on heavy tackle. Catch and release only — but the fight is unforgettable.
Blacktip & Spinner Shark✅ GOOD
Still working the nearshore beach zone. Cut bait under a float. Sight-casting on calm glassy mornings when conditions are right.
Kingfish✅ GOOD
Running beach zone with mackerel. Slow-troll live blue runners behind a #2 planer. Pick them up between reef runs.
Tripletail✅ GOOD
On crab trap floats and floating debris 2–4 miles out. Laying sideways near anything floating. Live shrimp free-lined next to structure. Excellent eating.
💡 PRO TIP

Hit the 1-mile reef for a quick snapper limit, run the beach for mackerel and a shot at beach tarpon on the way back in. Full cooler and possible bucket-list fish — entire trip under 3 hours.

🌉

Backwater & Bridge

▲
Tarpon buildingBackcountry baysSight fishing open

Tarpon in the backcountry bays and increasingly showing on the beaches. Snook moving to outside points. Clear water making gin-clear sight fishing a reality across the Ten Thousand Islands.

Tarpon🔥 HOT
Backcountry bays — laid up and rolling, eating well. 50–150 lb fish. Also showing on the beaches at first light. Live blue crabs under a float or large threadfin herring. Bigger concentrations building toward May peak. Approach quietly — stealth is critical. Strictly catch and release.
Snook🔥 HOT
Moving to outside points and islands ahead of summer spawn. Creek mouths and mangrove bends where current pushes bait. Gin clear water — trolling motor only, 100+ foot approaches. Topwater early, pilchards as sun rises. Incredible bite right now per multiple captains.
Redfish🔥 HOT
Pushing to open flats and island edges — visible tailing fish. Mix of redfish, trout, and jacks on the same flat without moving the boat. Best time of year. Peak month is right now.
Backcountry Sharks✅ GOOD
Lemon, bull, blacktip, bonnethead in backcountry bays. Cut bait on the bottom near deeper channel edges. Sharing water with tarpon — find one, find both.
Mangrove Snapper✅ GOOD
Docks, bridges, and structure throughout backcountry. Night fishing around lit docks especially productive. Small live shrimp on light fluorocarbon leader.
💡 PRO TIP

High sun mid-morning is when you can see furthest into the gin-clear water. That's your sight fishing window for snook and redfish on the flats. Use trolling motor only — start your approach 100+ feet away. Shadows and noise will spook fish in this clarity.
