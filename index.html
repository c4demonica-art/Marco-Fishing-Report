📋 OVERVIEW — April 19, 2026

Spring is hitting full stride around Marco Island this week with snook aggressive on the outside points and the first wave of migratory tarpon rolling along the beaches. Redfish and sea trout are stacking on warming flats, while sheepshead and mangrove snapper keep the backwater bite honest between tides.

🟢 Inshore

▲
Snook🔥 HOT
Aggressive on outside points and oyster bars. Live shiners and paddle tails on 3/8-oz jig heads are getting hammered on moving tides.
Redfish✓ GOOD
Tailing in skinny water on the top of the tide. Gold spoons and cut bait near mangrove roots producing slot fish.
Sea Trout✓ GOOD
Schooled over grass flats in 3-5 ft. Popping corks with shrimp or soft plastics remain the easy bite.

🌊 Nearshore (≤5mi)

▲
Tarpon✓ GOOD
First beach rollers showing up at sunrise. Threadfin and pass crabs on the incoming — numbers build into May.
Spanish Mackerel🔥 HOT
Busting greenbacks on the surface inside 3 miles. Fast-retrieved silver spoons and gotcha plugs on light wire leaders.
Kingfish✓ GOOD
Staging on nearshore wrecks. Slow-trolled threadfins with stinger rigs picking up keeper kings.

🌿 Backwater

▲
Sheepshead🔥 HOT
Stacked on dock pilings and bridge structure. Live shrimp on small jig heads during tide swings is the winning play.
Mangrove Snapper✓ GOOD
Holding over deeper creek structure. Shrimp or cut pilchards on a light fluoro leader for consistent snapper boxes.
Black Drum· SLOW
Scattered around oyster bars and residential canals. Fresh shrimp on bottom is still worth a soak on slack tide.
