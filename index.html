📋 OVERVIEW — April 23, 2026

Southwest Florida's spring bite shifted into a higher gear this week as water temps settled into the mid-to-upper 70s and bait pushed thick into the passes and along the beaches. Tarpon, snook, and redfish are leading the charge, with permit and amberjack stacking up on nearshore wrecks for crews willing to make the short run offshore.

INSHORE

▼
Snook🔥 HOT
Staged on outside points and oyster bars, feeding aggressively on the falling tide. Live pilchards and white flair-hawks producing slot-plus fish, with the best window the first two hours after the tide turns.
Redfish🔥 HOT
Schools cruising shallow flats on the morning low, singles tucked tight to mangrove roots. Cut ladyfish on the bottom, live shrimp under a cork, and weedless gold spoons all earning eats.
Spotted Seatrout✓ GOOD
Steady numbers on 3-to-5-foot grass flats with popping corks and live shrimp. Topwater walked at first light producing the better gator trout over four pounds.

NEARSHORE ≤5MI

▼
Tarpon🔥 HOT
Migrating schools strung along the beaches on calm mornings and stacking at the passes on tide changes. Live threadfins, pass crabs, and DOA Bait Busters all drawing eats — first light is gold.
Permit🔥 HOT
Early-season showing in big numbers on nearshore wrecks inside five miles. Live pass crabs on a 2/0 circle hook with minimal weight is the proven program.
Amberjack & Barracuda✓ GOOD
Same wrecks and ledges holding aggressive AJs and big toothy 'cudas. Vertical jigs and live blue runners getting hammered on the drop — bring heavy gear.
Goliath Grouper✓ GOOD
Warming water has them active on heavier structure inside 10 miles. Catch-and-release only — 80-pound class tackle minimum if you want a fighting chance.

BACKWATER

▼
Juvenile Tarpon🔥 HOT
Rolling consistently in Rookery Bay creeks and the Ten Thousand Islands backcountry. 10-to-40-pound class fish on light spin and fly — gulp shrimp and small streamers doing the damage.
Mangrove Snapper✓ GOOD
Reliable bent-rod fish around dock pilings and creek mouths. Live shrimp on a 1/0 hook with light fluorocarbon — keep it stealthy and they bite.
Sheepshead· SLOW
Tailing off as the water warms — most of the bigger spawners have moved out. A few stragglers still around pilings for anglers willing to scrape fiddlers.
