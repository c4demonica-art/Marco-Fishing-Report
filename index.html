📋 OVERVIEW — April 24, 2026

Spring is firing on all cylinders around Marco Island with water temps locked in the high 70s, calm mornings, and bait stacking along the beaches and passes. Early tarpon, pre-spawn snook, and tailing redfish are all in play right now, with the bigger tarpon pushes set to build end of April into May.

INSHORE

▼
Snook🔥 HOT
Pre-spawn fish moving to outside points and passes. Big white baits and live pilchards on the bottom during tide changes; night bite around bridges and dock lights fired up.
Redfish🔥 HOT
Prime month. Tailing fish on low-tide oyster bars and mangrove shorelines on the push. Gold spoons, weedless jerkbaits, and cut ladyfish on light spinning gear.
Spotted Seatrout✓ GOOD
Grass flats in 3-5 feet holding slot trout with a few gators mixed in. Popping cork with shrimp or a soft plastic on a 1/4 oz jig working best at the turn.

NEARSHORE ≤5MI

▼
Tarpon🔥 HOT
Migration strung along the beaches from Naples through Marco. Sight-cast live crabs or threadfins to rolling pods — best on the first two hours of light with flat seas.
Bull & Blacktip Sharks🔥 HOT
Following the tarpon in hard. Cut bonito or ladyfish on a steel leader, drifted just outside the passes, producing fish in the 6-8 foot class.
Spanish Mackerel✓ GOOD
Busting glass minnows within a mile of the beach. Silver spoons, small jigs, and gotcha plugs on the troll or cast to schools working birds.
Cobia✓ GOOD
Scattered singles cruising near markers and stingrays. Keep a pitch rod rigged with a bucktail or live pinfish ready on every nearshore run.

BACKWATER

▼
Mangrove Snapper✓ GOOD
Reliable bite on deeper docks, creek mouths, and shaded bridge pilings. Live shrimp on a knocker rig with 15-pound fluoro is the ticket.
Jack Crevalle✓ GOOD
Pods roaming the river and bay blowing up bait. Topwater walkers and loud plugs drawing savage strikes mid-morning through early afternoon.
Sheepshead· SLOW
Winter run winding down as water warms. A few stragglers still on barnacle-crusted pilings with live fiddlers, but bigger schools have pushed off.
