📋 OVERVIEW — April 22, 2026

Southwest Florida is firing on all cylinders this week as water temps stabilize in the mid-70s and bait pushes into the passes and along the beaches. Tarpon, snook, and redfish are the clear headliners, with permit and amberjack stacking up on nearshore wrecks for anglers willing to run a few miles out.

INSHORE

▼
Snook🔥 HOT
Staged on outside points, oyster bars, and shorelines. Live pilchards and flair-hawk jigs on the falling tide producing steady numbers, including slot-plus fish.
Redfish🔥 HOT
Prime spring window — schools cruising shallow flats at low tide, singles tight to mangrove roots. Cut ladyfish, live shrimp, and weedless soft plastics all working.
Spotted Seatrout✓ GOOD
Consistent numbers on 3-to-5-foot grass flats with popping corks and shrimp. Topwater at first light producing quality fish over four pounds.

NEARSHORE ≤5MI

▼
Tarpon🔥 HOT
Migrating schools visible along the beaches on calm mornings and stacking at the passes on tide changes. Live threadfins, crabs, and DOA Bait Busters all drawing eats.
Permit🔥 HOT
Early-season showing in big numbers on nearshore wrecks. Live pass crabs on a 2/0 circle hook with minimal weight is the ticket.
Amberjack & Barracuda✓ GOOD
Same wrecks and ledges holding aggressive AJs and big 'cudas. Vertical jigs and live blue runners getting slammed on the drop.
Goliath Grouper✓ GOOD
Warming water waking them up on heavier structure inside 10 miles. Catch-and-release only — heavy tackle required.

BACKWATER

▼
Juvenile Tarpon🔥 HOT
Rolling in Rookery Bay creeks and Ten Thousand Islands backcountry. 10-to-40-pound fish on light tackle — gulp shrimp and small streamers doing the job.
Mangrove Snapper✓ GOOD
Reliable bent-rod fish around dock pilings and creek mouths. Live shrimp on a 1/0 hook — keep the leader light.
Sheepshead· SLOW
Tailing off as water warms — the bigger fish have mostly moved out. A few still around pilings for anglers with fiddler crabs.
